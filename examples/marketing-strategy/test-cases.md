# Test Cases: Marketing Strategy Development

## Test Case 1: Consumer Product with Moderate Budget
**Inputs:**
- PRODUCT_DESCRIPTION: "EcoBottle - Smart water bottle, tracks hydration, $49.99, 100% recycled materials"
- TARGET_MARKET: "Health-conscious millennials/Gen Z, ages 22-35, tech-savvy, environmentally aware"
- BUDGET: "$50,000"
- TIMELINE: "3-month campaign, product available in 6 weeks"

**Expected Behavior:**
- Should identify multiple relevant channels
- Should allocate budget across channels strategically
- Should leverage social media heavily (target demographic)
- Should include influencer marketing
- Should have pre-launch and launch phases

**Success Criteria:**
- Budget allocation adds up to $50,000 (or close with contingency)
- Channels match target audience behavior
- Timeline shows phased approach
- Measurable KPIs defined
- Tactics are specific and actionable

## Test Case 2: B2B SaaS Product with Large Budget
**Inputs:**
- PRODUCT_DESCRIPTION: "DataSync Pro - Enterprise data integration platform, $999/month subscription, handles real-time data synchronization across multiple systems"
- TARGET_MARKET: "IT Directors and CTOs at mid-size companies (100-500 employees) in retail and e-commerce sectors"
- BUDGET: "$200,000"
- TIMELINE: "6-month launch campaign"

**Expected Behavior:**
- Should focus on B2B channels (LinkedIn, content marketing, webinars)
- Should emphasize thought leadership and education
- Should include account-based marketing approach
- Should focus on lead generation and nurturing
- Should avoid consumer-focused tactics (TikTok, Instagram influencers)

**Success Criteria:**
- Channel mix appropriate for B2B (LinkedIn, webinars, white papers)
- Metrics focus on qualified leads, not mass awareness
- Content strategy emphasizes expertise and ROI
- Sales enablement included
- Longer sales cycle acknowledged

## Test Case 3: Local Service Business with Small Budget
**Inputs:**
- PRODUCT_DESCRIPTION: "FreshBite - New organic meal prep delivery service in Austin, TX. Fresh, locally-sourced ingredients, customizable meal plans, $12-15 per meal"
- TARGET_MARKET: "Busy professionals in Austin, ages 25-45, health-conscious, value convenience"
- BUDGET: "$5,000"
- TIMELINE: "2-month launch"

**Expected Behavior:**
- Should focus on local/geo-targeted strategies
- Should leverage low-cost tactics (social organic, local partnerships)
- Should emphasize word-of-mouth and referrals
- Should use local influencers or food bloggers
- Should include grassroots tactics

**Success Criteria:**
- Budget-conscious approach (organic > paid)
- Geographic targeting emphasized
- Local partnerships and community involvement
- Realistic goals for small budget
- Creative, scrappy tactics
- Customer referral program

## Test Case 4: Luxury Product
**Inputs:**
- PRODUCT_DESCRIPTION: "LuxeTime Heritage Collection - Swiss automatic watches, $5,000-$15,000, limited edition of 500 pieces, luxury craftsmanship"
- TARGET_MARKET: "High-net-worth individuals, ages 35-60, watch collectors, appreciation for craftsmanship and exclusivity"
- BUDGET: "$100,000"
- TIMELINE: "4-month campaign"

**Expected Behavior:**
- Should emphasize exclusivity and craftsmanship
- Should use premium channels (luxury publications, private events)
- Should avoid mass-market tactics
- Should focus on quality over quantity
- Should leverage scarcity and prestige

**Success Criteria:**
- Premium positioning throughout
- Channels match luxury audience (not mass social media)
- Emphasizes exclusivity and limited availability
- May include events, partnerships with luxury brands
- Metrics focus on quality leads, not volume
- Storytelling about heritage and craftsmanship

## Test Case 5: Product with No Budget Specified
**Inputs:**
- PRODUCT_DESCRIPTION: "StudyBuddy - AI-powered tutoring app for high school students, helps with homework and test prep, $9.99/month"
- TARGET_MARKET: "High school students ages 14-18 and their parents"
- BUDGET: "" (not provided)
- TIMELINE: "Launch in 3 months"

**Expected Behavior:**
- Should create strategy without specific budget constraints
- Should prioritize tactics conceptually
- Should note that budget will determine scale
- Should provide a range of options (low to high cost)

**Success Criteria:**
- Doesn't invent a specific budget
- Provides strategy across channels without dollar amounts
- Notes budget will affect execution
- Prioritizes tactics by expected impact
- Provides both organic and paid options

## Test Case 6: Crisis/Repositioning Scenario
**Inputs:**
- PRODUCT_DESCRIPTION: "GreenClean - Eco-friendly cleaning products, relaunching after manufacturing issues resolved, rebuilding trust"
- TARGET_MARKET: "Environmentally conscious households, previously had 10,000 customers"
- BUDGET: "$30,000"
- TIMELINE: "Relaunch in 6 weeks, 3-month campaign"

**Expected Behavior:**
- Should acknowledge trust-rebuilding necessity
- Should emphasize transparency and resolution
- Should re-engage existing customers first
- Should address the past issue directly
- Should include reputation management

**Success Criteria:**
- Strategy acknowledges past issues
- Focus on transparency and rebuilding trust
- Prioritizes existing customers
- Includes testimonials and social proof
- Honest communication emphasized
- May include apology/explanation component

## Test Metrics

For each test case, evaluate:
1. **Audience Alignment**: Do tactics match target market behavior?
2. **Budget Management**: Does allocation make sense and add up?
3. **Completeness**: All required sections included?
4. **Specificity**: Are tactics actionable and specific?
5. **Measurement**: Are KPIs relevant and measurable?
6. **Realism**: Is the strategy achievable given constraints?

## Passing Criteria
- 6/6 must have tactics aligned with target audience
- 5/6 must have realistic budget allocation (where budget provided)
- All must include specific, measurable KPIs
- All must have actionable tactics (not just vague ideas)
- Channel selection must be justified by audience/product
- Timeline must be logical and phased
